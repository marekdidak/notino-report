01 — Strategic core

Define the category before anyone else does

Croatia is not a market to enter cautiously — it is a market to define. No global booking platform is present. Notino already has strong brand love among Croatian consumers. The challenge is not awareness or competition: it is converting a culturally walk-in-first, community-oriented salon market into one that sees digital booking as an income tool, not an administrative burden. The strategy is to own that narrative now, before global players arrive with bigger budgets.

"We are not another booking platform. We are the first platform that pays Croatian salons for the client relationships they already have — through the commissions their clients generate on Notino every day."

Key advantage: Notino is a recognised love brand in Croatia from B2C. Unlike Austria, this brand recognition can and should be leveraged — the B2B message bridges from "you already know us" to "now we pay you back."

💰

Income Generator — not Admin Relief

Zoyya's core positioning is operational: accounting integration, fiscal cash register compliance, admin automation. Notino's positioning is the opposite end of the value chain — passive income. "We pay you" is a message Zoyya cannot credibly make. Lead with it in every channel.

🌍

The only bridge to Western EU tourists

90.7% of overnight stays in Croatia are foreign tourists — German, Austrian, Italian — who currently have no effective way to book local salons in their language. Notino's multilingual platform is the only product that solves this. No Croatian competitor can match it.

🆓

Free, with no Zoyya-style dependency

Zoyya's stickiness comes from fiscal register integration — salons feel locked in. Notino Partner is free to join and adds revenue on top of whatever system they already use. Frame it as additive, not disruptive. Lowering the switching barrier is the fastest path to adoption.

🏖️

April–May acquisition window is non-negotiable

Coastal salons enter their peak in June. Any salon that isn't registered before the summer season starts will not join mid-season. The entire acquisition push for coastal markets must land in April and May. Missing this window means waiting until September.

The single biggest hurdle

Walk-in culture and the fear of lost flexibility. Field research reveals that a significant portion of Zagreb salons — particularly outside the city centre — operate on a walk-in or informal phone basis. The objection to online booking is not technical: it is existential. "What if I'm full and a walk-in comes?" is the most common concern. The strategy must reframe online booking not as a replacement for walk-ins but as an additional income layer that works alongside existing habits. The self-onboarding journey and all content must address this friction directly and early.

02 — Seasonal acquisition calendar

Croatia runs on a different clock than Austria

Croatia's salon market is shaped by tourism seasonality in a way Austria's is not. Coastal salons are at capacity June through August — they will not evaluate new platforms mid-season. Zagreb and inland markets are relatively stable year-round. The acquisition strategy must respect this rhythm entirely.

Apr–May
Coastal acquisition window

Critical. Every coastal salon that will join in 2025 must be acquired before June. Heavy push on Split, Dubrovnik, Hvar, Rovinj. OOH goes live. Tourist pipeline messaging front and centre.

Jun–Aug
Zagreb acquisition + coastal hold

Peak tourist season. Coastal salons are busy — maintain brand presence but do not push hard acquisition. Double down on Zagreb and inland markets. Registered coastal salons begin generating B2C bookings from foreign tourists.

Sep–Oct
Back-to-business reminder wave

Season ends on the coast. Coastal salons re-engage with business decisions. Second acquisition push with summer results as social proof: "Here's what salons earned this summer on Notino." Referral programme amplification.

Nov–Mar
Zagreb sustain + event seeding

Always-on performance channels. Beauty and Hair Expo Zagreb (January) for Tier 2 pipeline. Webinar series. Engagement campaigns for registered salons. Content production for next summer's push.

The April–May window is the most important decision in the entire Croatia plan. A salon that misses the June start will not see meaningful B2C tourist bookings until the following summer. If budget must be cut, cut autumn spend before cutting the April–May coastal push.

05 — The tourist moat

The competitive advantage no Croatian platform can copy

90.7% of overnight stays in Croatia are foreign tourists. The majority come from Germany, Austria, and Italy — countries where Notino is an established and trusted consumer brand. These tourists cannot currently book Croatian salons in their language, cannot find them through familiar channels, and cannot pay in ways they trust. Notino's multilingual booking platform solves all three. This is a structural advantage no local competitor can replicate.

The tourist pipeline argument in one sentence for salons: "Every German tourist who wants a haircut in Split has no way to find you today. After joining Notino, they book you directly — in German, through a brand they already trust." This argument closes coastal salons faster than any commission conversation.
Why this works for coastal salons
  • German, Austrian, and Italian tourists already use Notino at home — they trust the platform and are familiar with the booking flow
  • Tourists arriving for 7–14 days specifically seek beauty services (hair, nails, spa) as part of their holiday experience
  • Coastal salons currently turn away tourist demand they cannot capture — this is real lost revenue, easy to quantify with the Summer Tourist Demand Estimator tool
  • No Croatian platform (Zoyya, SrediMe, Fresha) offers multilingual booking or has brand recognition with Western EU tourists
EU funding lever — HBOR/ERDF
  • The €170M Croatian sustainable tourism loan programme (HBOR/ERDF) funds digitalisation investments for tourism-adjacent businesses
  • Premium coastal salons already applying for or receiving these funds are actively upgrading their digital infrastructure
  • Position Notino Partner as the booking and income layer that completes their digitalisation — complementary to, not competing with, their existing upgrade investments
  • This angle is most effective with Tier 2 premium coastal operators. Include in the country manager's direct outreach script for this segment.

Surface intent through tools, not content

Croatia's community-oriented salon culture means word-of-mouth and peer validation carry more weight than polished brand content. Lead magnets must feel useful and personal, not corporate. Every tool should generate a number that the salon owner wants to share with a colleague — that is how referral begins organically before the formal referral programme launches.

07 — Activation & the walk-in objection

Convert registration into active use — and answer the fear

Two challenges are unique to Croatia compared to Austria: the walk-in culture objection must be addressed proactively in the onboarding journey, and the gap before B2C booking goes live (September) must be filled with enough value that salons do not disengage. The activation programme handles both.

Addressing the walk-in objection — in the funnel, not after

The objection: "What if I accept online bookings and then a walk-in client comes and I have no space?" This is the most common reason coastal and Zagreb salons hesitate to adopt any booking platform. It must be addressed in the self-registration landing page, in the onboarding email sequence, and in all group call Q&As — before it becomes a reason to disengage.
How to frame it in landing page copy
  • "You decide how many slots to open online. The rest stays walk-in." — make the control explicit and immediate in the registration flow
  • "Notino doesn't replace your walk-in clients — it adds the ones who can't find you today" — reframe from replacement to addition
  • Show a simple schedule mockup where 3 slots are "online" and 5 slots are blank (walk-in available) — visual proof that the salon retains control
How to handle it in the onboarding sequence
  • Week 2 email subject: "Kako uskladiti online rezervacije i walk-in klijente" — address the objection as a practical tutorial, not a defence
  • Include a real example from a Croatian salon partner who started with 2 online slots per day and expanded gradually — peer validation is the most persuasive format in this market
  • Group call Q&A: always have a prepared answer ready for this question — it will come up every session without exception

Post-registration activation timeline

Week 1
Personal contact from the country manager

A phone call — not an email, not automated. Croatian salon owners respond to personal contact. The call covers: profile completeness, photo setup (Notino photographs every salon personally), and a brief demo of what the tourist estimator shows for their specific location. This call is the single most important conversion touchpoint in the entire Croatia funnel.

Weeks 2–8
Weekly email sequence — practical, not promotional

Each email delivers one action: set your availability slots (with walk-in guidance) / link your Notino product profile / set up your referral link / see your commission tracker. Week 4 email for coastal salons specifically: "Ljetna sezona počinje za X tjedana — evo kako privući turiste koji već traže tvoj salon." Creates urgency without pressure.

M1–ongoing
WhatsApp Early Access community — Croatian conversational format

"Notino Partner Hrvatska — Early Access" group. Entry via post-registration page and personal invite from the country manager. Weekly prompt: "Koji je tvoj omiljeni Notino proizvod koji preporučuješ klijentima?" — surfaces product recommendation behaviour that directly drives commission income. Monthly voice note from the country manager answering real questions from the group. The community format fits Croatian communication culture far better than formal email sequences.

September
B2C booking goes live — use summer as social proof

For salons registered before June, September brings real booking data. Use this immediately: "Saloni koji su se pridružili u travnju ostvarili su prosječno X turističkih rezervacija ovog ljeta." This is the most powerful acquisition content for the autumn push and the referral programme launch. Real numbers from real Croatian salons beat any marketing message.

Month 6
Referral programme activation with summer data

Launch the €50 referral programme in September armed with summer results. "Preporuči kolegu i oboje zaradite" is a stronger message when salons can show their own summer booking numbers. Croatian peer networks are tight — one satisfied coastal salon owner will actively recruit colleagues without financial incentive if the experience was good. The programme amplifies what is already happening organically.

What not to do: Do not offer financial incentives to drive early registrations. Do not use automated phone outreach or chatbots for the Croatian market — field research confirms this culture responds negatively to impersonal contact. The country manager's personal availability (including evening calls) is a genuine competitive advantage here and should be protected as a resource.

09 — Execution model

The country manager is the Croatia advantage

Croatia's community-oriented culture means the country manager's personal presence — in person in Zagreb, by phone in the evenings for the rest of the country — is a disproportionately high-leverage asset. This is different from Austria, where walking outreach was primarily a multicultural-district play. In Croatia, personal contact is the primary conversion mechanism across all segments.
