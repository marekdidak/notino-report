01 — Strategic core

Own the undigitised majority

Austria's real opportunity is not in fighting for the 7% of salons already on a booking platform. It is in the 93% that have never adopted one — and who don't yet realise that their existing clients are already active Notino shoppers generating revenue that could be flowing back to them. The strategy is to make that connection concrete, personal, and impossible to ignore.

"We are not a booking tool. We are the only platform that pays your salon every time your client buys a product online — from a customer base they already have."

Key assumption: Notino's B2C brand recognition in Austria cannot be relied upon to accelerate B2B adoption. All spend is planned as if entering a completely unknown market.

💰

Revenue Growth Partner — not booking software

Lead with passive income, not operational efficiency. The 5% commission on Notino e-shop purchases is a differentiator no competitor can match. Every piece of creative anchors on incremental revenue the salon is already leaving on the table.

🆓

Genuinely free — no hidden conditions

Fresha's "free forever" collapsed under a 20% marketplace fee. Treatwell charges 35% on the first client. Notino Partner is free with no commission on existing clients. Communicate this plainly and repeatedly — it is a structural competitive advantage.

🎯

Attack pain without naming competitors

The feel-good brand guideline prohibits direct attacks. The message is affirmative: "Join for free. No commission on your first clients." Any salon that has dealt with Treatwell or Fresha will understand the implication without it being stated.

Undigitised salons, no competitor exposure

All layers live from month one

The funnel runs concurrently — not in sequence. Awareness spend creates a pool of salon owners who have seen Notino Partner. Performance channels must be running the same week to capture that pool before inertia sets in. Budget weighting shifts across the year; channel activation does not.

The core principle: Awareness spend without a running conversion funnel is wasted. Google Search and Meta go live on day one — even at modest spend — to capture any existing demand. Retargeting begins at month 2 once the pixel has sufficient data to build a meaningful audience.

05 — Multicultural lever

The 46% opportunity competitors ignore

46.3% of Vienna's population has a migration background, with Turkish and Balkan communities forming the largest groups. Salon owners from these communities run a significant share of Vienna's undigitised barbershops and beauty salons — and they are systematically under-reached by every competitor's digital campaigns.

Key insight from field research: The overwhelming majority of these salon owners speak German fluently and are actively reachable in German. The opportunity is not in language — it is in channel, creative representation, and physical presence in the right districts. Campaigns run in German with culturally representative visuals and in-person outreach.

07 — Lead magnets

Surface intent, don't educate

Every lead magnet answers a question the salon is already asking, and makes Notino the logical conclusion. The framework: the magnet must surface buying intent and collect a contact — not simply inform. PDFs and whitepapers do not meet this bar.

01

Mobile-first web tool at notino.at/partner/revenue-calculator. Three inputs: monthly clients, product recommendation rate, average basket value (pre-filled with Austrian defaults). Output: estimated monthly Notino commission in euros.

08 — Activation & the 2-month gap

Keep registered salons engaged before B2C goes live

The gap between B2B registration (June) and B2C booking going live (September) is the most dangerous window in the plan. Salons that register and see zero booking activity for two months will churn before the product delivers its core value. The activation programme makes them feel like insiders — not early adopters waiting for a product.

Week 1
White-glove onboarding call

Personal call from the country specialist — not automated. Focuses on profile completeness, photo quality (Notino photographs every salon personally), and setting expectations for the September launch. This call prevents the "I registered but nothing happened" churn pattern.

Weeks 2–8
Weekly email sequence — one action per email

Each email delivers one practical insight and one action: optimise your Notino product page / set up your referral link / check your commission tracker / invite a peer. Final email in the sequence: a preview of what the first month of B2C bookings could look like based on their profile completeness score.

M1–ongoing
WhatsApp Early Access community

"Notino Partner Österreich Early Access" — entry only via post-registration Danke page and personal invite from the country specialist. Weekly prompt from Notino: "Teilt euer Lieblingsprodukt der Woche — wir zeigen, wie ihr damit mehr Provision verdienen könnt." Monthly live voice note answering three common questions from the group. Community is intentionally diverse: Turkish and Balkan barbers, central Vienna salons, mix of categories — so screenshots and testimonials show real variety.

Month 5
Referral programme activation

Launch the €50 partner referral programme only once a base of satisfied early partners exists. Asking people who haven't experienced value to vouch for the product undermines trust. By month 5, the weekly emails and community engagement create a cohort ready to advocate.

September
B2C booking goes live — insider moment

Communicate the launch to registered partners before it goes public. Frame it as: "As an early partner, your profile is live before the general announcement." First-mover visibility is real — salons listed first in any category receive higher organic traffic. Make the early partners feel they won by joining early.

What not to do: Do not offer financial incentives (free credits, bonus commissions) to drive early registrations. Buying signups creates a cohort that registered for the incentive, not the product — they don't complete profiles, don't confirm bookings, and produce a poor client experience at launch. The activation programme earns engagement through genuine value.

Distinctive B2B brand assets

Master message — all channels

"Your clients already shop at Notino. Now you get paid for it." — in German: "Ihre Kunden kaufen schon bei Notino. Jetzt verdienen auch Sie daran." Used consistently across OOH, digital ads, influencer content, and the self-registration landing page.

Visual differentiation from B2C

B2B creative uses a structured layout within the feel-good palette — more businesslike, less lifestyle. All social content features real Austrian salon owners filmed on location, not stock imagery. Authenticity is the primary differentiator.

Diverse representation

Creative assets intentionally show a mix: Austrian-origin stylists, Turkish barbers, Balkan-origin salon owners. Not as a diversity initiative — as an accurate reflection of who actually runs Vienna's salons.

The commission dashboard as hero asset

The single most persuasive piece of content is a real salon owner showing their actual Notino commission tracker. This is the creative brief for all Tier 2 and 3 creator content. No produced animation beats real numbers on a real screen.
