Vienna — the anchor market
8.17 million guests in 2024 (+3.3% vs 2023), 82% foreign, 18% domestic. Vienna has relatively low seasonality variation vs comparable European cities, providing year-round demand. Vienna's foreign-born population is 40.9% (2025), foreign nationals 36.4%, people with migration background 46.3%. Language use: 16.4% speak only German at home; 48.7% speak German + another language with friends. 73% of Austria's population speaks English (Eurobarometer).

Price sensitivity
61.9% of consumers prioritize affordability amid economic pressures. 34% switch brands for lower prices (especially body care). Highly price-sensitive consumers: 45.5%. Moderately price-sensitive: 36.4%. Typical promotional discounts: 10–20%. However, 35% of Austrian consumers are willing to pay premium for sustainability. Nail care premium segment captures ~60% of global revenue.
Austrian legal context — no-show & cancellation
Supreme Court case 4 Ob 63/21z: declared even carefully drafted no-show clauses void if "surprising and disadvantageous" or "grossly disadvantageous" for consumers. Clauses requiring 100% payment for no-shows without considering re-booking are at risk. Strong requirement to deduct saved costs (if slot refilled).

Tiered cancellation model (standard Austrian):
30+ days before: Free
30–14 days: 50% fee
14–7 days: 80% fee
<7 days: 90–100% fee
If replaced by another guest/customer, fee must be reduced (no double-payment).

Notino implication: Build cancellation workflows that comply with Austrian consumer protection law. Avoid aggressive no-show fees that could be legally challenged. Focus on reminder-driven no-show reduction combined with optional deposits.

01

The commission guilt

Salon owners already recommend products to clients every day — verbally, informally, for free. They create demand they never capture. The emotional tension: "I'm already doing this. Why am I not getting paid for it?" This is not about greed — it's about recognition of invisible labour.

02

Earning while you sleep — what does it actually mean for a solo operator?

A solo salon owner works 10-hour days on their feet. "Passive income" sounds like a fantasy from a different world. The creative challenge: make the 5% commission feel real, tangible, and earned — not like a gimmick. Show the notification on a phone at 11pm. Show the monthly total next to a grocery receipt. Ground it in their actual life.

03

Paper diary vs. commission dashboard

The visual contrast between old and new. A handwritten appointment book (still used by most Austrian salons) next to a phone showing real commission income. Not mocking the old way — honouring it while showing what's missing. "Your diary tracks appointments. This tracks income you didn't know you had."

04

The Treatwell scar

Many salon owners have been burned by platforms that promised exposure and delivered commission pain. Treatwell's 35% on first clients, Fresha's bait-and-switch from "free forever." The creative must acknowledge this history without naming names. "We know you've heard 'free' before. Here's why this time is different."

The multicultural identity tension

Turkish and Balkan salon owners in Vienna navigate between their cultural community and the Austrian business environment every day.
