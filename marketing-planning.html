Q1
What is the single primary goal for year one — salon count or revenue?
Key context: Volume vs quality optimisation require completely different strategies and incentive structures.
What it unlocks: KPI framework · Campaign optimisation signal
Q2
What is Notino's risk appetite on the Planity clock?
Key context: Planity lands in Austria in 6–12 months with €46M and a 0%-commission model.
What it unlocks: Go-to-market urgency · Budget phasing · AT vs HR priority
Q3
Will Notino invest in dedicated B2B brand building, or ride the consumer brand?
Key context: Salon owners currently see Notino as a cosmetics shop, not a business partner.
What it unlocks: Content strategy · Social channel architecture · Budget split
Q4
Which market gets prioritised first — Austria or Croatia?
Key context: Half-effort in both risks winning neither; each has a different urgency driver.
What it unlocks: Resource allocation · Hiring plan · Campaign calendar Q2–Q4 2026
Q5
What is the acquisition motion — outbound push or inbound pull?
Key context: SEO and events take 6–18 months; Planity arrives in 6–12.
What it unlocks: Channel mix · Team structure · 90-day vs 12-month horizon
Q6
What launch incentive can Notino offer — and for how long?
Key context: Undigitised salons start at zero bookings — the free tier threshold is a barrier.
What it unlocks: Onboarding offer design · Activation rate · Campaign hook
Q7
Is there a referral or community strategy, or purely direct acquisition?
Key context: One well-connected salon owner in Vienna or Zagreb unlocks dozens at near-zero CAC.
What it unlocks: Ambassador programme · Referral mechanic · Event activation
Q8
What is the monthly budget available for B2B acquisition — per market?
Key context: Budget determines whether the acquisition motion is field sales, paid media, content, or all three.
What it unlocks: Channel mix · CAC ceiling · Hire vs outsource decision
Q9
What acquisition channels has Notino already tested in existing markets — and what happened?
Key context: Any prior outreach, even informal, contains signal about what messaging and channels work.
What it unlocks: Channel shortlist · Avoid repeating failures · Faster iteration
Q10
Who owns B2B acquisition — and what is their capacity?
Key context: Strategy without an executor is a document. Founder-led, dedicated sales, or agency changes everything.
What it unlocks: Realistic target-setting · Execution plan · Outsourcing decisions
Q11
What does the onboarding journey look like today — and where does it break?
Key context: Marketing can only hand off a warm lead; if onboarding leaks, pipeline volume won't fix it.
What it unlocks: Funnel design · Drop-off diagnosis · Activation rate baseline
Q12
Are there any partnership, distribution, or co-marketing channels available?
Key context: WKO (Austria) and craft associations (Croatia) can unlock dozens of salons per conversation.
What it unlocks: Partner channel strategy · CAC reduction · Trust shortcuts
